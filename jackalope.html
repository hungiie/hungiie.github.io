This project aims to introduce a new mobile app, Jackalope, that is catered towards the needs of the international students demographic in Canada.

Why Jackalope?

OUR AI MASCOT

A mythical creature symbolizing the unknown and the boundless possibilities of starting anew in a different country.

Like the Jackalope - an unknown, unique blend of rabbit and deer - it represents the merging of diverse cultures and lifestyles that await international students in Canada.

It’s a reflection of the exciting journey of discovery and connection.

Domain research

Primary users

  • International students in Vancouver in their 20s.
  • Recently arrived in Canada for university or college.
  • Seeking opportunities to connect with peers, explore local culture, and engage with their interests.
  • Diverse cultural backgrounds with diferent levels of English proficiencies.

    • Challenges faced by internation students

  • Difficulty finding events or communities that match their interests.
  • Lack of centralized platforms catering specifically to their needs.
  • Overwhelming information scattered across multiple platforms or word of mouth.
  • Lack of detailed and personalized guidance to adjusting to the cultural shock in Canada, leading to social isolation.

    • → Missed opportunities

  • Local communities and event organizers often struggle to reach the international students demographics.

  • Many students miss valuable networking and social opportunities, which are crucial for their academic and personal success.

    • Common goals

  • Want to minimize the time they take to adjust to the cultural shock when arriving to Canada.
  • Motivated to build a sense of belonging in their new environment.
  • Find friends and communities with the same interests.

    • What we want to achieve with Jackalope

    → Foster a sense of community for international students by providing a platform that allows a user to search for different events and engage in communities based on their interests.

    → Assists users in getting used to the Canadian cultures/ norms, minimizes cultural shock with useful tips generated by AI for each event.

    → Problem statement

    "How can we help international students build community, find events, and connect with interest-based groups while minimizing the initial cultural shock?"

    Interaction design

    Supported platform

    Mobile App Interface - Users primarily interact through mobile app on iOS and Android platforms.

    Input methods

    Tap interactions:

  • Profile Creation: Tapping to select interests, languages, and preferences.
  • Community Joining: Tapping buttons to join communities.
  • Event RSVP: Tapping "Attend" or "Interested" for upcoming events.

    • Type interactions:

  • Search Function: Typing to find specific communities or events.
  • Messaging: Messaging other users or groups.
  • Profile Customization: Typing personal bio and additional details.

    • Other input methods:

  • Swipe: Swiping through AI’s outputs
  • Scroll: scrolling through different events and communities.

    • Features

    INTERESTS-BASED MATCHING

    Users can select different areas of interests upon creating their profile.

    These would then influence how much priority an event has when being shown to the user.

    These interests would also be shown publicly on a user’s profile page. This would allow users to see and connect with other users if they have similar interests.

    EVENTS DISCOVERY

    The main feature of the app is the events and communities. Users can see the details of the event, contact the event organizers with any questions the user has, and RSVP/save an event if their interested.

    Both of these are central features of Jackalope because they give the user a good understanding of the events and communities that are available to them and if they fit the their interest.

    AI ASSISTANCE

    Jackalope, our AI assistance can generate different useful tips based on each event, that will prepare international student for any potential cultural shock

    If the user finds these tips unsatisfactory, they have an option to “dislike” the answer. This would bring up the option for them to contact the event organizers or for them to ask the community, or provide feedbacks to Jackalope.

    COMMUNITY MESSAGING

    In-app messaging for direct communication between users and communities, or event organizers.

    Accessing the AI feature

    The AI feature was designed to:

  • Help reduce cultural shock for students.
  • By providing contextual explanations for unfamiliar words or phrases.

    • Initially, the idea was that users could select a word or phrase they didn't understand, and the AI would explain it in the appropriate context.

    However, this approach had the following issues:

    Limited Proactivity The AI's support was passive, requiring users to activate it manually. This reduced its ability to assist users effectively.

    Unintuitive Interaction Highlighting text to request an explanation felt unnatural, making it difficult for users to recognize this as a viable interaction option.

    EARLY HEURISTIC EVALUATION

    Consistency & Standard: Not carefully considerated. Straight slidebar design gives the false impression that users can drag the bar (very standard for streaming platforms, such as YouTube, Netflix, etc.), but in reality the bar in card.io is only to signify the progress, not to be interacted

    The slidebar is a very standard visibility status indicator in streaming apps, which users can use their fingers to drag them to their desired point.

    USABILITY TESTING & INTERVIEWS

    Understanding runners' behaviours

    I conducted 2 separated usability testing with 2 participants

    “I barely ever put my phone in my pocket. I would rather put it on the treadmill so I can see what song is playing at that moment.”

    The user keeps their phone visible on the treadmill, not in their pocket, for easy interaction and information access.

    This suggests the phone is stationary but at arm's length.

    Values visual confirmation of the current track and possibly other playback details like time remaining or tempo.

    Likely wants minimal distractions but appreciates visibility and accessibility.

    “If I don’t like the song, I will change to the next one - it’s kinda hard though. Since I was running, having to press my finger on a single point on the screen is hard and I sometimes missed the “next” button.”

    Small touch targets (e.g., the “next” button) are difficult to press accurately during physical activity.

    The user desires an effortless and distraction-free way to control and access information while running.

    Likely values simplicity, ease of access, and minimal effort in interacting with the app.
